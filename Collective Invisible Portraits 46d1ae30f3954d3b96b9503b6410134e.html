The Idea

  • Provide an judgement free space for people to fail
  • Let people fail in a fun and free environment
  • Blind Drawings allow people to fail with the expectation of failure
  • Cushions the consequences of failing by introducing humor
  • Saving the photos allows me to document and share failure to help others embrace failure

Low Level Prototypes

  • Began at paper prototypes
  • Tested with two people drawing each other
    • Realized that people tunnel vision on the face instead of looking elsewhere
    • 30 second timers added constraints to force failure

Installation Setup Prototyping

  • Physical to Digital Drawing
    • Started as an installation including two laptops and using machine learning to draw
    • Iterated towards using touch screens for drawing
    • Moved forward to using Wacom tablets | Were easier to setup and gave the feeling of actually drawing
  • Installation Setup
    • Wanted to find an easy method for people to see the drawings of themselves
    • Began with putting monitors behind both artists | Felt too bulky and uneccesary
    • Tried connecting the tablets to the opposite laptop | Problems with desync and confusion
    • Asked artists to switch seats after | Felt like the easiest method of sharing the imagery

Coding Prototypes

  • Started very simple with getting drawing working
  • Moved towards getting a menu setup for people to understand the prompt
  • Added camera to allow people to see their face compared to the drawing upon suggestion.
  • Added a timer to create constraints and to also reveal the drawing after timer
  • First full prototype
  • Added options to the end of the drawing such as reset and photo | Also added text to associate the person with the drawing
  • Updated menu
  • Completed full iteration

Reflection

After the demo in class, I realized that while the experience was extremely rough, it still tapped into the joyful emotion that I was aiming for. Making it clear for what users need to do at what points is extremely difficult without guidance and trying to find an easy method of leading them has been a struggle. I still think the overall experience is a success outside some much needed debugging for things like colors. I want to try to improve the experience before the show especially make the instructions and what the people are supposed to do very clear. I don't think the instructions are as clear as they could be. I will update my reflections upon doing more demonstrations and the winter show.

After hearing about the format changes from the show due to covid, I withdrew my project from the show. I will point out that I did get infrastructure to save all photos to a google drive for participants to save the photos of them and I think that had people excited. I think showing the gallery of photos is something that I thing would definitely worth including in documentation. After some more user testing, I feel like I have a good balance between p5 but also practical participation AKA switching seats.
