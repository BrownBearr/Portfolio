What is the Replasticing Project

Replasticing is both a class and an initiative sponsored by the NYU Makerspace to try to turn the excess PLA plastic into something new and divert them from entering the waste pipeline.

As a part of the class, we were able to access to the NYU makerspace and take on specific projects to try to tackle the issue of excess PLA.

I joined the Sheets Team, a group dedicated to melting PLA scraps into sheets which can then be used much like acrylic.

3 Stage Sheet Process

  1. Measure
  1. Melt
  1. Cool

Using this simple but effective process, we can melt scrap PLA into sheets that can then be repurposed.

Production

These sheets have the ability to be treated much like acrylic

This goes to say that they can be bandsawed and laser cut!

With laser cutting the sheets, we can now make them usable prototyping material for students that was initially scrap/trash

Students are able to express creativity and prototype using this easy to use material.

Some of the benefits of this material are:

  1. Mistakes can be remelted into new sheets
  1. Cheap
  1. Any miscuts can be easily solved with a heat gun
  1. Wonderful color variety
