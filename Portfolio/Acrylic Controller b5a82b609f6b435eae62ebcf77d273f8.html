The Idea

  • Make a controller so that I can play video games better.
  • Fighting games like street fighter or tekken require precise inputs which this controller will help me perform
  • Make it small low profile, but easy to manufacture for myself

References

Technical Prototypes

  • Used a Rasberry Pi Pico on recommendation from a friend
  • Used basic Pcomp knowledge to buy and understand it
  • Referred heavily to FeralAI and his GP2040 Documentation and discord
  • Began by adding headers to the pi pico
  • Using the basis for the pi pico, used GP2040 a gamepad firmware developed by FeralAI. Check out his discord above or
  • https://github.com/FeralAI/GP2040
  • gp2040.info
  • From the help of the references and FeralAI and his discord community

Physical Prototype

  • Wanted to create a mixbox style controller (refer to the full controller images above).
    • They use mechanical key-switches to control and lead to more quicker and precise controls compared to a fightstick.
  • First tested with seeing what size holes I needed to cut for the switches
  • Began measuring the size holes that would be the perfect size for the key switches.
    • Settled on a size of .535 inches
  • Began trying layout
  • After initial tests, Began cutting clear acrylic with the layout I had defined before.
  • Complications: I did not create enough gap between the switches to fit keycaps on them properly. I had to go back and laser cut a new piece of acrylic with a new layout with more gaps between switches

Creating the Controller

  • Now that I had a full physical enclosure and a working Pi Pico, I could build the controller
  • Started by placing all the switches into the acrylic plate and then soldering
    • Soldered a singular wire between 1 pin of every switch and then soldered wires between the other switch pin to the corresponding microcontroller pin to get the correct input
  • With everything soldered, I could use the controller to create inputs
